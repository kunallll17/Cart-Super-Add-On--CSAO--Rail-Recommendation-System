\section{Ideation \& Problem Formulation (15\%)}
% =============================================================================

\subsection{Mathematical Framing}

We formalised CSAO recommendation as a \textbf{Session-Based Next-Basket
Ranking} problem --- not binary classification --- because the
\emph{position} of an item on the CSAO rail directly determines its
click probability.

\medskip
\textbf{Formal definition.}
Let $S = \langle i_1, i_2, \ldots, i_t \rangle$ be the ordered sequence of
items added so far. Learn a scoring function:
\[
  f_\theta\!\left(i \mid S,\, u,\, r,\, \tau\right) \;\in\; \mathbb{R}
\]
over candidates $i \in \mathcal{C} \subset \mathcal{M}_r$ conditioned on
cart state $S$, user $u$, restaurant $r$, and temporal context $\tau$.
We optimise \textbf{NDCG} via \textbf{LambdaRank}:
\[
  \lambda_{ij} \;=\;
  \frac{-\sigma}{1+e^{\sigma(s_i - s_j)}}
  \cdot \left|\Delta\text{NDCG}_{ij}\right|
\]
The $|\Delta\text{NDCG}_{ij}|$ weight ensures gradients are largest for
swaps that would most hurt ranking quality --- directly optimising
the rail's primary business signal.

\subsection{Offline Embedding Pipeline (The AI Edge)}

\textbf{Step 1 --- Food Knowledge Graph + PMI.}
Build weighted directed affinity graph $G=(V,E,w)$:
$|V|=788$ items, edges boosted by \textbf{PMI co-occurrence} signals.\\
Edge weight:
$w(i \rightarrow j) =
  w_{\text{cat}}(i,j)
  \cdot (0.5 + 0.5\cdot\text{pop}_j)
  + 0.30\cdot\mathbf{1}[\text{cuisine}_i = \text{cuisine}_j]
  + 0.15\cdot\text{PMI}(i,j)$

\medskip
\textbf{Step 2 --- Message-passing embeddings.}
Initialise 12-d structural features, project to 128-d, run 20 iterations:
\[
  \mathbf{h}_v^{(t+1)} =
  0.6\,\mathbf{h}_v^{(t)} +
  0.4\,\frac{\sum_{u\in\mathcal{N}(v)} w(v,u)\,\mathbf{h}_u^{(t)}}
             {\sum_{u\in\mathcal{N}(v)} w(v,u)}
\]
In production, \textbf{GPT-4o-mini} descriptions replace
$\mathbf{h}_v^{(0)}$ for richer food-domain grounding.

\medskip
\textbf{Step 3 --- FAISS IndexFlatIP.}
L2-normalised embeddings \TO{} Inner Product $\equiv$ cosine similarity.
Exact ANN retrieval in \textbf{0.223 ms}.

\section{System Design \& Production Readiness (15\%)}
% =============================================================================

\subsection{Architecture Overview}

The system follows a \textbf{two-tower retrieval + re-ranking} architecture.
The offline tower builds semantic item embeddings via graph message-passing;
the online tower constructs cart-contextual features and scores candidates
through LightGBM LambdaRank.
Architecture stages with measured latencies:

\begin{archbox}
\begin{lstlisting}
OFFLINE PIPELINE  (runs nightly)
  GPT-4o descriptions -> Text Embeddings -> Graph Message Passing
  -> L2-normalise -> FAISS IndexFlatIP
  -> item_embeddings.npy + faiss_item_index.bin

ONLINE SERVING  (FastAPI + Uvicorn, artefacts pre-loaded at boot)

  POST /recommend
    |
    +-- Stage 1: Mean cart embedding -> FAISS.search(k=50)   0.223ms
    |   Returns 50 semantically compatible candidate IDs
    |
    +-- Stage 2: Feature construction (cart ctx x 50 items)  3.863ms
    |   (production: Redis feature store reduces to <1ms)
    |
    +-- Stage 3: LightGBM.predict (21 trees, 43 features)    0.476ms
    |   argsort -> top-K ranked items
    |
    +-- Stage 4: JSON serialisation                           ~0.1ms

  Total mean: 4.56ms  |  p99: 7.01ms  |  Budget: 200ms
\end{lstlisting}
\end{archbox}

\section{Model Evaluation \& Fine-Tuning Strategy (15\%)}
% =============================================================================

\subsection{Temporal Train/Test Split --- Zero Leakage (v2)}

The dataset is split strictly by \textbf{session\_timestamp}
(true chronological order, not an \texttt{event\_seq} proxy):
the earliest 80\% of sessions form the training set,
and the most recent 20\% form the test set.
A 10\% validation slice (by time) is carved from the training end
for early stopping.
\textbf{No session or user appears in both splits} --- zero leakage.
All normalisers (MinMaxScaler, LabelEncoders) are fit exclusively on
training data and applied to the test set --- eliminating target leakage,
recency bias, and future-data contamination.

\textbf{Negative candidate sampling:} For each cart event, 4 same-restaurant
negative items are added to create 5-item query groups
(1 positive + 4 negatives), giving LambdaRank realistic pairwise gradients.

\textbf{Error analysis:}
0\% of queries produce zero NDCG@5;
44.6\% achieve perfect ranking;
38.6\% in the ``good'' range (0.5--1.0).
Performance is consistent across meal times (Breakfast 0.71, Dinner 0.74,

(low 0.67 \TO{} high 0.76).

\medskip
\textbf{Known limitation:}
The 44.6\% perfect-ranking rate is a known artefact of \textbf{small
query groups} in synthetic evaluation (1 positive + 4 negatives = 5 items
per group). With only 4 negative candidates, LambdaRank needs to place
the single positive above just 4 alternatives --- a tractable task that
overrepresents NDCG@5 = 1.0.
In production with 50 FAISS candidates per query, we expect the
perfect-ranking fraction to drop to 15--25\% and mean NDCG@5 to
stabilise around 0.55--0.65, which remains a strong lift over baselines.

\subsection{Fine-Tuning Strategy}

\begin{itemize}[noitemsep, leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Online learning:} Nightly retrain on rolling 90-day window
    using confirmed add-to-cart signals as LambdaRank labels.
  \item \textbf{Embedding refresh:} Weekly FAISS rebuild, or triggered
    immediately on menu changes (new item \TO{} partial graph update).
  \item \textbf{HPO:} Optuna TPE over \{num\_leaves, lr, min\_child\_samples\},
    NDCG@5 objective, 50 trials, 3-fold time-series CV.
  \item \textbf{Cuisine-specific models:} Long-term: per-cuisine-cluster
    LightGBM rankers for sharper complement pattern capture.
\end{itemize}

\end{metricbox}

\subsection{Rigorous A/B Testing Design}

% FIX: reduced first column to 3cm and wrapped in \small to prevent overflow
{\small
\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{>{\bfseries\RaggedRight\arraybackslash}p{3cm} >{\RaggedRight\arraybackslash}X}
  \toprule
  Component & Design \\
  \midrule
  Assignment unit &
    User-level (not session-level) to prevent contamination.
    Bucket via $\text{MD5}(\text{user\_id}) \bmod 100$. \\[3pt]
  Traffic split &
    50/50 Control (popularity rail) vs.\ Treatment (CSAO LambdaRank). \\[3pt]
  Ramp schedule &
    Day 1--3: 5\% canary. Day 4--7: 20\%. Day 8+: 50\% if no
    guardrail breach. \\[3pt]
  Primary metrics &
    (1)~CSAO Attach Rate --- fraction of sessions with $\geq$1 add-on added.
    (2)~Average Order Value (Rs./session).
    (3)~Revenue per Session = AOV $\times$ C2O. \\[3pt]
  Statistical design &
    Two-sided $t$-test ($\alpha=0.05$, power=0.80). MDE = 1\% AOV lift.
    Required sample $\approx$130{,}000 sessions/arm ($<$2 days at 2M/day). \\[3pt]
  Guardrail metrics &
    Cart Abandonment Rate must not increase by $>$0.5 pp.
    Time-to-Checkout must not increase by $>$5 s.
    Refund Rate must not increase. \\[3pt]
  Novelty effect &
    Measure primary metrics separately for Days 1--7 and Days 8--21. \\
  \bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
}

\subsection{Guardrail --- Cart Abandonment is the Kill Switch}

\[
  \text{CAR} =
  \frac{\text{Sessions with cart but no order}}
       {\text{Total sessions with }\geq 1\text{ item added}}
\]

If $\text{CAR}_{\text{treatment}} - \text{CAR}_{\text{control}}
> +0.5\,\text{pp}$ with $p < 0.01$, the experiment is auto-paused
and the model is rolled back immediately. The recommendation system
must \emph{never} cannibalise the core ordering funnel.

\begin{metricbox}

\textbf{Live API} --- \texttt{POST /api/recommend} to FastAPI at \texttt{localhost:8000}. Real FAISS retrieval and LightGBM ranking; response includes \texttt{retrieval\_ms}, \texttt{ranking\_ms}, \texttt{total\_ms}, \texttt{candidates\_pool}. \\

\subsection{Integration Flow}

When in Live mode, the frontend sends a JSON payload to \texttt{POST /api/recommend} with: \texttt{user\_id}, \texttt{restaurant\_id}, \texttt{cart\_items} (item IDs, quantities), \texttt{order\_hour}, \texttt{is\_weekend}, \texttt{slot} (Breakfast/Lunch/Tea-Time/Dinner/Late-Night), and \texttt{user\_lifetime\_orders}. The API returns ranked recommendations with \texttt{item\_id}, \texttt{item\_name}, \texttt{category}, \texttt{base\_price}, \texttt{score}, \texttt{why}, plus latency breakdown. The UI displays up to 5 cards and shows the total latency in the ``Recommended in Xms'' badge.
